## ğŸŒ The Problem

### The Crisis
In many parts of India, vulture populations have crashed. Historically, vultures removed most animal carcasses quickly and prevented diseases like anthrax, rabies, and botulism from spreading through rotting meat.

### The Impact
Without fast carcass cleanup:
- Dead animals lie in the open for longer
- Increased disease risk for wildlife, livestock, dogs, and humans
- Disrupted nutrient cycling in the food web

## ğŸ¯ Our Solution

### Goal
Build a field-ready robot that mimics a vulture's sanitation job by finding dead matter, mechanically shredding it into small pieces, and adding enzyme solution to accelerate safe decomposition into compost-like output.

### Core Idea
Instead of eating carcasses like vultures, the bot uses **sensors**, a **shredder**, and an **enzyme-spray/tank system** to turn hazardous dead matter into partially decomposed, easier-to-handle material within hours to days.
