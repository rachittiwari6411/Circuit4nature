Vulture Bot – Environmental robotics project

Problem Statement

The vulture crisis

Vultures are important decomposers. When animals die, vultures help clean up the carcasses and reduce the spread of disease.

In many areas, vulture populations are dropping because of habitat loss, poisoning, and human activity. This means carcasses stay longer in the open and can become a health risk for other animals and people.

Our idea: Vulture Bot

Vulture Bot is a small robot that tries to copy part of the vulture’s job in a simple, controlled way. It focuses on detecting dead animals and helping handle them more safely.

The goal is not to replace real vultures but to explore how technology can support public health and the environment when a key species is in danger.

Objectives

  • Detect carcasses using basic computer vision (YOLO-style models).
  • Move towards the carcass using a Raspberry Pi based control system.
  • Use a small arm or scoop to interact with the carcass area.
  • Test simple methods to reduce smell and exposure safely.
